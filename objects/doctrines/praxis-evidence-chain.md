## Doctrine

Praxis treats workflow continuity as an evidence chain, not as a chat transcript or a tool-specific memory trick.

A human or agent should be able to answer:

1. What was intended?
2. What instruction was issued?
3. What boundaries applied?
4. What work happened?
5. What evidence proves it?
6. What remains unsafe, incomplete, blocked, or deferred?
7. What is the next safe movement?

## Authority boundary

Evidence does not equal authority.

A public object may explain how Praxis works. It does not grant permission to access private systems, change private code, release software, publish private material, or perform operational work.

## Practical rule

When in doubt, preserve:

- the instruction,
- the boundary,
- the evidence,
- the result,
- the next safe movement.

Do not preserve private operational details in public Praxis unless they have been explicitly scrubbed and approved for public use.
